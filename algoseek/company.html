  • Cheese, AI and
    Quality Data

    There are many different cheeses in the market, because people need different flavors.

    On the contrary, milk is a relatively "standard" product with a consistent flavor. There are many different dairy brands because each producer has limited lands and cows and relatively fixed variable costs.

    News service is like cheese; Ultra low latency market data is similar to milk.

    Historical market data is neither cheese nor milk. There are good data and bad data, there are expensive data and fairly-priced data, but the market doesn't need many flavor varieties for historical data. And the variable costs is close to zero.

    There are lots of historical market data vendors out there mostly because of information asymmetry. Data is hard to compare. People don't know which is best choice.

    But the era of Al and machine learning is coming.

    ML needs simplicity. ML also needs sophistication. A real data expert knows what are relevant and what are not. All the mistakes, data holes, inconsistencies, etc., will be exposed under the exhaustive scrutiny of algo testing and machine learning.

    Al is still stupid, but will not be stupid forever.

    When the inflection point comes, vendors of quality data will shine. This article is not really about vision. This is about our understandings and beliefs.

  • The Mission
    Impossible

    Worldwide, millions of tickers are trading or had been traded. Tens of gigabytes of trades and quotes information are generated every minute.

    Tickers change. ISINs change. Names change. Primary exchanges change. Merge. Spin off. Incept. Bankrupt. IPO. Delist. The market is forever dynamic. Nobody yet has recorded accurately all information.

    Future, option, bond, forward, SWAP, warrant, unit, preferred. There are many derivatives centred around every company, every forex pair, every commodity, etc. Many information have been lost in the ocean of history, by both human mistakes and machine mistakes. Upon close inspection, historical data is messy. You cannot count on writing codes to solve all data problems.

    Maths is cool. Data is grinding.

    We manually deal with missing data, conflicting information and outliers. It takes extreme attention to details. It takes extreme sense of responsibility.

    How to become a quality data vendor:

    • 1. Make sure your data is well organized and ready for use;
    • 2. Make sure every piece of information is right and accurate.

    Easy to say, hard to do. Mission impossible to do it month after month and year after year.

    When idealism meets reality, we fight against perfunctoriness and indolence like Don Quixote.

    It is our mission to accomplish the mission impossible.

    The passion burning inside our chest is like a never-extinguished bonfire.

    We take the grinds, and you stay cool.
