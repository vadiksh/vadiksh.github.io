Your vs you’re

 

à "Your" is the second person possessive adjective. It is used to describe something as belonging to someone. It should be followed by either a noun or the gerund.

Where is your hat?

Your being here has been very helpful.

 

à "You’re" is the contraction "you are." It is usually followed by the present participle, but can also be followed by an adjective.

You’re going to be late!

I wonder if you’re getting sick.

It seems like you’re hungry.
