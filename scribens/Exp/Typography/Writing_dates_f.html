Writing dates

 

àWhen and where commas are used for dates depends on the information provided.  When a phrase contains only the month and year, no punctuation is needed.

 

I visited Vietnam in May 2011.

 

Signe graduated from high school in June 2007.

 

àWhen the date of the month is also included, there must be a comma before the year. 

 

The ceremony took place on May 17, 2001.

 

I arrived in France on August 26, 2015.

 

à Furthermore, if the day of the week is included, there is a comma after it.

 

The grocery store will be closed Wednesday, July 13, 2016.

 

à There are a variety of ways to write a decade.  The important thing to remember is avoid using two apostrophes.

 

Acceptable formats include:

 

1980’s

 

1980s

 

The ‘80s

 

The 80’s

 

*Remember to avoid something like the ‘80’s
