Past perfect

 

à The past perfect is used to show that one event happened before another one. It doesn’t matter which event is mentioned first in the sentence; the past perfect clarifies their temporal order.

 

Using the past perfect

 

First event

Second event

I had left

Before Shelly arrived at the office.

Clara had done the dishes

When I returned this morning.

I had just boarded the airplane

When my mother called me.

 

à Rules for conjugating past perfect:

•         Paste tense of have + past participle of main verb

•         It’s often used with specific expressions of time, like:

o   After, before, by the time, etc.

àExamples:

 

After I had called the hotel I saw you.
