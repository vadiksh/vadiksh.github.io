Conditional sentences tenses

 

à Conditional phrases can be used in past, present, and future; however, they more importantly break down into two categories: real and unreal. As the names suggest, the real conditional describes real situations, while the unreal conditional describes imaginary situations.

 

à Conditional sentences are always composed of two clauses. The conditional clause (that begins with "if") shouldn’t contain "would" or "will."

 

If + conditional clause tense,

+ main clause

This is used to describe what you would generally do in an imaginary situation.

Future unreal conditional
