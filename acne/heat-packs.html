  • Backed by science

    Wondering how it really works? Let’s break it down.

    Just before menstruation, our hormones cause a compound called prostaglandin to tighten up the uterine muscles, in an effort to expel the uterine lining a.k.a period blood. The pain you feel during this time is literally “cramps”. And that’s where heat comes in! Heat relaxes muscles and brings blood vessels back to their normal state, making it one of the most natural and effective ways to deal with pain.
